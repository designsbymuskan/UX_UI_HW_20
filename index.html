Non-Profit Website Redesign Case Study: NABA.org

About This Project

The NABA Redesign Project was a collaborative effort, where our team worked together to transform the online presence of the North American Butterfly Association into a captivating and user-centric experience.

Overview

The NABA redesign project aims to transform the online presence of the North American Butterfly Association into a captivating and user-centric experience. Through user research, we will gather insights on pain points and challenges faced by stakeholders of NABA, and aim to solve these through a website redesign.

  • Increased engagement and involvement with conservation efforts, including volunteer opportunities, donations, and sponsorships.
  • Users are able to document butterfly sightings and promote a sense of community through sharing and social media

Our Roles

  • Our team embraced a dynamic and collaborative approach throughout the NABA Redesign Project. While traditional roles might not have been defined, we actively engaged in various facets of the project to ensure its success.
  • Collectively, we tackled challenges that arose during the project, adapting as needed to deliver results. For instance, one team member spearheaded research paper creation when stakeholder interviews proved challenging to obtain. Others focused on problem statements and design solutions.
  • Our strength lay in our ability to work harmoniously and pivot as a group to meet the project's evolving needs. This flexibility allowed us to harness each team member's unique skills and expertise to create a comprehensive solution for NABA.

Hypothesis

We hypothesize that a redesign of the current NABA website, focussed on increasing human connection and trustworthiness, will help users feel more inclined to donate their time and money to the non-profit.


Research

In the next phase of our project, we conducted a comprehensive research effort to validate our initial hypothesis. This included in-depth user interviews to gather firsthand insights into their needs, preferences, and challenges. Additionally, we used research papers and publications from sources like Google Scholar to supplement our knowledge. We also formulated a research plan to guide our approach, objectives, and data collection methods.

User Insight

Through in-depth user interviews and research papers from Google Scholar, we gained valuable insights into user needs, preferences, and challenges, shaping our project direction.

Problem Statement

It was from this wealth of data and user feedback that we identified a clear problem statement. Our research illuminated a central issue that required addressing to enhance NABA's online presence. This problem statement became the foundation for our project, guiding our design and development efforts.

Iteration 1

To ensure an exceptional user experience for NABA, we actively sought and embraced user feedback. One significant iteration involved our homepage design. Initially, we included a prominent donation pop-up to encourage financial support. However, after user interviews, we adjusted our approach based on feedback: users preferred exploring and learning about the organization before encountering donation requests. As a result, we reduced the prominence of the donation pop-up and focused on providing a clear and engaging introduction to NABA's mission and offerings. This change underscores our commitment to user-centered design, enhancing the website's effectiveness.

Iteration 2

We made a significant improvement on the quiz page, responding to user feedback that indicated an opportunity to enhance engagement. Initially lacking visual elements, we added small images and icons, enriching the user experience and increasing engagement.

Prototype

As a culmination of our efforts, we developed a high-fidelity prototype that embodies the culmination of our design solutions. This prototype not only reflects the iterative improvements made throughout the project but also serves as a tangible representation of our user-centric approach
